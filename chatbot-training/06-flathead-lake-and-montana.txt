BIG SKY PARASAIL - FLATHEAD LAKE AND MONTANA INFORMATION

=== ABOUT FLATHEAD LAKE ===
Flathead Lake is the largest natural freshwater lake west of the Mississippi River, located in northwest Montana. It is approximately 28 miles long and 15 miles wide, covering nearly 200 square miles. The lake is renowned for its incredibly clear water — visibility can reach 30+ feet in many areas.

The lake sits at an elevation of approximately 2,893 feet above sea level and is surrounded by mountains on all sides, including the Mission Mountains to the east and the Salish Mountains to the west. Maximum depth is approximately 370 feet.

Flathead Lake is fed primarily by the Flathead River, which flows from Glacier National Park. The lake's waters are exceptionally clean and cold, with summer surface temperatures typically ranging from the mid-50s to low 70s°F.

=== THE FLATHEAD VALLEY ===
The Flathead Valley encompasses the communities surrounding Flathead Lake, including Kalispell, Whitefish, Bigfork, Polson, and Lakeside (where Big Sky Parasail operates). The valley is a premier destination for outdoor recreation, offering:

- Water sports on Flathead Lake (boating, kayaking, paddleboarding, fishing)
- Hiking and mountain biking in surrounding mountains
- Glacier National Park access (west entrance about 40 miles from Lakeside)
- Whitefish Mountain Resort for skiing, hiking, and mountain biking
- Wild Horse Island State Park on Flathead Lake
- Cherry orchards along the east shore (cherries ripen in July)
- Art galleries, fine dining, and craft breweries
- Golf courses with mountain views
- Horseback riding and ranch experiences

=== GLACIER NATIONAL PARK ===
Often called the "Crown of the Continent," Glacier National Park is approximately 40 miles north of our marina. The park features:
- Over 700 miles of hiking trails
- 26 named glaciers (shrinking due to climate change)
- Going-to-the-Sun Road — one of America's most scenic drives
- Pristine alpine lakes and waterfalls
- Diverse wildlife including grizzly bears, mountain goats, and elk
- The west entrance is accessible from Kalispell/Columbia Falls

=== WILD HORSE ISLAND ===
Wild Horse Island is the largest island on Flathead Lake, covering approximately 2,165 acres. It's a Montana State Park accessible only by boat. The island is home to:
- Small herd of wild horses (descendants of horses placed there by the Salish-Kootenai tribes)
- Bighorn sheep
- Mule deer
- Bald eagles
- Pristine hiking trails with panoramic lake views

Big Sky Parasail offers a Wild Horse Island Adventure charter for $2,000 (4 hours, up to 12 guests).

=== NEARBY TOWNS ===

Lakeside, MT (home base):
Small lakeside community on the west shore of Flathead Lake. Home to Flathead Harbor Marina where Big Sky Parasail operates.

Kalispell, MT (15 miles):
The largest city in the Flathead Valley. Shopping, dining, medical facilities, and the gateway to Glacier National Park. Home to Glacier Park International Airport (FCA).

Whitefish, MT (25 miles):
Popular resort town known for Whitefish Mountain Resort, charming downtown, breweries, and restaurants. Year-round outdoor recreation hub.

Bigfork, MT (20 minutes):
Charming village on the northeast shore of Flathead Lake. Known for art galleries, the Bigfork Summer Playhouse, fine dining, and a beautiful downtown area.

Polson, MT:
Located at the south end of Flathead Lake. Home to the Flathead Lake Brewing Company and the gateway to the Mission Mountains Tribal Wilderness.

=== MONTANA WEATHER (SUMMER) ===
Montana summers (May-September) are ideal for parasailing:
- Daytime highs: typically 70-90°F (21-32°C)
- Morning lows: 45-55°F (7-13°C)
- Low humidity compared to coastal areas
- Long daylight hours (up to 16+ hours in June)
- Afternoon thunderstorms possible but usually brief
- Morning hours (9-10 AM) tend to have calmest lake conditions
- UV index can be high at Montana's elevation — sunscreen is essential

Montana sunrise/sunset times for Flathead Lake area (approximate):
- May: sunrise ~6:00 AM, sunset ~8:45 PM
- June: sunrise ~5:30 AM, sunset ~9:30 PM
- July: sunrise ~5:45 AM, sunset ~9:20 PM
- August: sunrise ~6:20 AM, sunset ~8:30 PM
- September: sunrise ~7:00 AM, sunset ~7:30 PM

=== TIME ZONE ===
Big Sky Parasail operates in the Mountain Time Zone (MDT during summer, UTC-6).
